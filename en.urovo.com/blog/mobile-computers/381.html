Your Guide to Buying a Mobile Computer for Your Business

2021-10-12 14:20:00
Source：Urovo

Mobile computers are a technology's blessing to teams in a variety of industries. They are effective, easy to use, and above all, portable for day-to-day business activities. Integrated with IoT systems, mobile computers can benefit your business in countless ways.  

 

You may wonder, how do I choose the right mobile computing solutions for my business?

 

Do not worry! In this guide, we will introduce you to the different types of mobile computers. Then, we will discuss the main factors you need to consider when opting for PDA solutions. 


 


Mobile Computers Introduction

A mobile computer is as important to your business as a smartphone is to your personal life. Business owners can save themself and their employees a ton of stress by getting a mobile computer to run their business effectively. Ultimately, there are different types of mobile computers for various business purposes.

 

Types of Mobile Computers

There are different types of mobile computing designed to make your work more efficient. Usually, these devices are classified based on design outlook and grip type:

 

● Handheld Terminals

Handheld terminals look like smartphones or come out a bit larger. They are mostly used for scanning barcodes, GPS tracking, voice communication, data recording. UROVO RT40 and DT50 5G are wonderful examples of  mobile devices.

 

The RT40 is a simple handheld device for scanning over a range as long as 15 miles. It comes with an anti-condensation screen and a 5200mAh battery that can be hot-swapped for unhindered efficiency.

 

On the other hand, the DT50 5G is a touch screen mobile device without keypads. As 99% dustproof and waterproof, this device could be the most efficient ally for your business. There is an attachable ergonomic handle that can be used for a gun-grip of the device. The equipment main utilization is making large-scale scanning of products.

 

● Wearable Terminals

Wearable devices can be attached to the body for as long as you need them to be. They have all the features of a typical mobile computer and are great for ensuring productivity in the workplace. With wearable mobile terminals, your hands are free to perform tasks smoothly.

 

● Vehicle Mount Terminals

These are also referred to as fixed-mobile devices. They are perfect for your day-to-day running with forklifts. Since they are most likely not used for product scanning, they do not come with scanners. Choosing a vehicle mount terminal is all about rightly assessing what your business needs. Vehicle Mount Terminals are designed to suit several purposes such as documentation, tracking, grouping, and dispatching products.

 

● Tablet Computers

Tablet computers are optimal for in-house assessments. They provide a broader and more detailed view of your products and make drag-and-drop actions easier. Some of them come with pens that allow you to input data manually. UROVO P8100 Enterprise series is an example of a handheld tablet; it has an Android 10.0/9.0 operating system for optimum performance. Other utilizations are related to high-end scanning, GPS tracking, communication, long-lasting power, and resilience.

 

Factors to Consider when Buying a Mobile Computer

Your business has a unique work environment, which means it must make use of proper devices. Several factors come into play when deciding what mobile computer to buy:

l Size of your organization

l Complexity of duties performed

l Climate

l Number of technical manpower available

l Technological setup  

 

The organization's needs are not to be ignored when choosing devices. Those computers you buy should be able to serve your business and improve productivity. They should be durable enough and prove valuable for the money invested in them.

 

The following are important suggestions to keep in mind when buying mobile computers:

● Your Work Environment and the Users

What is the nature of your business? Do you work on rugged fields, or are you restricted to the warehouse? Are you a small enterprise or a large-scale organization? These are questions you should answer to ensure you get the proper devices. Your workplace and the users will determine the ruggedness and durability of the devices you should buy.

 

● Industry Standards and Specifications

For every mobile computer, the manufacturers have limits and ranges. You would not want to buy a device that will be performing much more than the manufacturers designed it to. This situation could lead to malfunction and, at worst, a breakdown of the device. Before buying a computing device, check the features, specifications, and limits.

 

Is the connectivity strength worth the money? Is the scanning power good enough for you? Can the tablet withstand shock from dropping on hard surfaces?

 

The certifications, guarantees, and customer reviews on a particular device can help you determine these cogent matters.

 

● Operating System and Configuration

You might not have thought this to be important, but the configuration settings and operating system affect the efficiency of your devices. An excellent mobile computer should have little or no problem troubleshooting. In addition, the device should have a stable operating system.

 

Other features such as remote controlling, automatic software updating, and IT support are great for getting your devices to perform to their best.

 

● Device Flexibility

Over time, your organization will experience expansion or improvement in modus operandi, which may require a change in operational procedure. You will want to get devices as flexible as possible to support significant changes in your organization without becoming obsolete.

 

Computing devices may be costly, and their ruggedness does not require changing year-in, year-out. You can prevent piling up old-fashioned devices in your warehouse by buying flexible ones.

 

Provisions like software updating and integrated imaging allow a mobile computer to meet the criteria for different working conditions as your organization expands and improves.

 

● Battery Capacity

Do not buy a mobile computer without considering the battery capacity of the device. Most mobile computing operations drain a lot of power. If you are a field worker who may not have ready access to electricity, your devices should be as efficient as possible where battery capacity is concerned.

 

The higher the battery capacity, the better for you. You can consider how much time in a day you will spend using a particular device and estimate the amount of power that will drain. It is recommended that you opt for computers that won't require charging until the day's work is over.

 

Better quality equipment costs higher, but you will reap their long-term benefits in the long run.


 


Conclusion

Following these guidelines should help you buy a suitable mobile computer for your business. When making a purchase, remember that durability is essential. Therefore, you should opt for devices manufactured by companies you trust in their ability to design rugged products.
